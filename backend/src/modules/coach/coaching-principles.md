Apply these powerlifting programming principles as guidelines — respect them wherever the requested
day count and goal allow, but the schedule and the lifter's data come first.

Frequency (when the number of days per week allows): squat about 2x/week, deadlift 1-2x/week, bench
press 3-4x/week. Fill any remaining days with overhead pressing and accessories chosen to bring up the
lifter's weak points and assist the main lifts.

Recovery and spacing: leave roughly 48 hours before training the same lift or muscle group heavy again,
and never schedule two HEAVY sessions of the same main lift on back-to-back days. When a lift is trained
twice in a week, make one session a heavier low-rep day and the other a lighter higher-volume day.

Periodization: start the plan lighter and higher-volume (around RPE 6-7) and ramp target RPE upward over
the weeks toward the end of the plan. For plans of 6 weeks or more, insert one lighter "deload" week
(reduced volume, ~RPE 6) before the hardest week. Progress the main lifts week to week by raising
targetRpe and/or trimming reps — the app converts RPE into the actual kg load, so the RPE ramp IS the
progressive overload.

Keep weekly volume sustainable and bias accessory selection toward the posterior chain and the lifter's
weak points.

## strength
Main lifts mostly 1-6 reps. Ramp toward RPE 8-9 (an occasional 9.5 late in the plan). Keep accessory
volume moderate and focused on the competition lifts.

## hypertrophy
Most working sets in the 6-12 rep range at RPE 7-9, with higher set counts. Frequency guidance still
applies, but prioritize total volume over maximal intensity; do not chase RPE 10.

## peaking
Taper accessory and overall volume as intensity climbs. Insert a deload the week before the peak. In the
final week, prescribe heavy low-rep top sets (singles or doubles) ramping to RPE 9-10 — a true near-max
effort on the competition lifts — with accessory work sharply reduced.

## general_fitness
Balanced full-body training, mostly 5-10 reps at RPE 6-8. Favor consistency and recovery over maximal
intensity; do not push sets to RPE 10.
